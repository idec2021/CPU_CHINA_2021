PROLOGUE

The semi-rational directed evolution approach in protein engineering remarkably increase the efficiency of enzyme remolding. In our project, we adopted computational software capable of performing QM and MD calculations, as well as machine-learning algorithms to evolve our enzyme, which facilitated the generation of small but targeted library. This strategy not only lessen the iterations to identify desired variants, but also largely eliminated the need for high-throughput methods in library analysis.

MODELING

Initially, molecular dynamic simulation was performed to determine sets of amino acid residues with the greatest impact on the thermostability of wild-type MnP (wtMnP). By interpreting results of RMSF, B-factor and other indices reflecting secondary structure alteration of MnP at different temperature conditions, residues with their position around 228-233 were selected for the subsequent construction of single mutation library by FoldX. PLMC related analysis was also conducted in parallel to support the result produced by FoldX. Later, Turi Create was applied to predict the "scaled_effect" of MnP, and Rosetta was used to correct the mutation free energy of mutation points generated by FoldX to improve the reliability of the data. Eventually, 10 mutants with the smallest ΔΔG were selected for stability verification.

MUTANT SCREENING

Based on the original nucleotide sequence of manganese peroxidase, 4 out of 10 selected optimal mutants (serial number 1# 2#, 7# and 8#) were successfully generated on shuttle vector pPIC9K by applying site-directed mutagenesis, and were transferred into Pichia pastoris for heterogenous protein expression, followed by assaying the enzym distinct temperature, pH and organic solvent conditions. As a result, mutant 8# showed a significant activity improvement under low pH condition, while mutant 7#’s organic solvent stability was remarkably improved.
