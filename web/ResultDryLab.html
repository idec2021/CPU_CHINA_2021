MOLECULAR DYNAMIC SIMULATIONS ANALYSIS

To discover the innate structural properties of manganese peroxidase (MnP), we operated molecular dynamic simulation analysis on Gromacs.

RMSD and RMSF analysis

First, though the analysis of the track file of MD simulation, RMSD [1] curve (Fig. 1) was obtained, and the curve revealed the change of protein backbone stability in four temperature gradients of 298.15 K, 313.15 K, 338.15 K and 400 K. Higher the RMSD value, poorer the stability of protein backbone. As shown in Fig. 1, with the increase of temperature, the backbone stability of MnP gradually decreased.

Fig. 1 Average RMSD value of MnP at different temperatures.

 

We also obtained the RMSF curve (Fig. 2) under four reaction temperatures of 288.15K, 313,15 K, 338.15 K and 400 K through the analysis of the track file of MD simulation. The curve revealed that it was mainly position 228-233 residues of MnP that were most sensitive to temperature, thus facilitating the later construction of single mutation library. Meanwhile, we compared the RMSF[2] curve with the B-factor plot (Fig. 3) from Bio3D, and the filtrated range of thermo-sensitive residues on the two figures was almost the same.

Fig. 2 The RMS value of each residue of MnP at different temperatures.

Fig. 3 MnP B-Factor plot.This curve reflects the thermostability of each amino acid residue of the MnP.

Secondary structure and salt bridge alterations

The secondary structure of MnP was also simulated and the results revealed through the Ramachandran plot and salt bridges.

As shown in Fig. 4, the white area shown in the Ramachandran plot shrank with the increase of temperature, which represented the disruption of MnP's structure[3].

Fig. 4 MnP Ramachandran plot at different temperature. This is a graphical illustration for visualizing protein backbone energetic position in terms of torsion angles ( & ).

Moreover, its secondary structure ( -sheet part especially) was severely damaged (Fig. 5), while the number of salt bridges increased with the rise of temperature which could be seen from the programming output. The above three indices reflected the disruption of protein structural stability caused by the increase of temperature.

Fig. 5 MnP secondary structure changes with temperature.

OPTIMIZATION OF MUTANT LIBRARY

Rosetta

After building the library by FoldX[4] , we made mutation free energy corrections for the beneficial mutation points that have been screened through Rosetta for a second database. Afterwards, Funclib was used to build a multi-functional enzyme library[5] (Table. 1) with Rosetta[6,7] . Here we only considered the sites near the substrate binding pocket of the enzyme, and constrained the key active side chains and ion conformations, which could narrow the sequence sampling space to an exhaustive range. Through the combination of softrepack, repack, rotmin and other commonly used movers, we completed the scoring of the mutants. Later all the sites were exhaustively combined, and tolerable mutations were obtained as candidate mutation types. Finally, by sorting and clustering all the results, suggestions for mutants were obtained to expand the substrate selectivity of 3M5Q.

Table. 1 Calculated amino acid residue sets at available mutation sites under different ΔΔG values.

DL & ML analysis

We constructed the sequence library of the mutated enzyme, predicted[8] the "scaled_effect" (Fig. 6) generated by the mutation with Turi Created applied, and merged it into the final .fasta file, which is used as the benchmark data set for deep learning (DL) training. According to the weight file generated after the calculation, support vector machines (SVM), random forest[9] , and K-nearest neighbor (KNN) were used to score the model to obtain the mutation activity of our enzyme, and the Spearman coefficients were accordingly 0.86, 0.88 and 0.92, which proved the result of “scaled_effect” after applying Turi Create was fine.

Fig. 6 MnP scaled_effect.The scatter points in the figure are composed of the first principal component of PCA and the second principal component of PCA. Variant effect's color reflects the confidence of scaled_effect.

While such process was carrying on, we calculated the ectopic dominance of the protein after mutation through PLMC, and expressed the distance matrix (Fig. 7) between its amino acids through the MATLAB code construction, which was helpful to better understand the effect of mutation on the surface of the protein.

Fig. 7 MnP PLMC module analysis and the distance matrix of amino acid resideues.
