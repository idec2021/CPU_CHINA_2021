OVERVIEW

Nature has provided us with tremendous enzymes that can mediate multiple essential biochemical processes delicately, but they are not always suitable for the industrial world. Never rest content with their current abilities, protein engineering has the potential to optimize the characteristics of enzymes and expand the scope of their application. Herein, we attempted semi-rational directed evolution strategy to improve the stability of MnP from Phanerochaete chrysosporium. Computational redesign brought an array of potential mutants, and 10 of them were further heterologously expressed in Pichia pastoris to screen out a better one with high rubostness.

 

MANGANESE PEROXIDASE: THE INITIAL ENZYME

Manganese peroxidase (MnP) is a highly glycosylated, heme-containing[2] lignin peroxidase produced by the white-rot fungus Phanerochaete chrysosporium. MnP catalyzes the hydrogen peroxide-dependent oxidation of Mn2+ to Mn3+, which is released from the enzyme in complex with oxalate, enabling the oxalate-Mn3+ complex to serve as a diffusible redox mediator capable of oxidizing lignin or other refractory chemicals[2,3] (Fig. 1).

 

Fig. 1 Catalytic cycle of manganese peroxidase. [1]

However, the activity and stability of MnP is remarkably influenced by pH, temperature, organic solvents and incubation time. Sutherland and Aust et al. found that MnP from P. chrysosporium was most stable at pH 5.5, and at temperature equivalent to or below 37℃ [4]. Thus, it could not fulfill the manifold requirements in industrial biotechnology, which reflects the necessity of its directed evolution for a broader usage.

 

MOLECULAR DYNAMIC SIMULATION

To discover specific mutation sites of MnP that could enhance its thermostability and activity, the main software we adopted in the molecular dynamic simulation was Gromacs, and we employed it for RMSF & RMSD analysis. Comparing the RMSF curve with the static B-factor curve obtained by bio3D, the thermo-sensitive amino acid residues were screened out.

 

CONSTRUCTION AND OPTIMIZATION OF MUTANT LIBRARY

We used FoldX to construct our mutated enzyme library and acquired mutation free energy, ΔΔG, for each mutation point. Based on this we utilized Rosetta to rectify the free energy and a library with multi-function enzymes was constructed according to the spatial position of the ligand.

MUTANT CONSTRUCTION AND PARTIAL PROTEIN PURIFICATION

Using pPIC9K as the bearer of sequences of MnP and the mutants, recombinant Pichia pastoris strains were constructed. This very expression system has the following advantages :

  • Pichia pastoris is a methylotrophic yeast which can use methanol as the sole carbon source.
  • Pichia pastoris has protein folding, post-translational modification, protein glycosylation and other functions that make it an excellent eukaryotic expression system.
  • Pichia pastoris has fast, simple, low-cost and higher level of expression characteristics
  • Combined with pPIC9K plasmid, cells can express exogenous proteins at high levels under methanol induction.

After the inducible expression for 72h, the medium supernatant was collected by centrifuging at 15000 rpm for 30 min twice, removing all the insoluble impurities. Thus the crude protein solution were obtained.

 

MUTANT STABILITY ASSAY

Three kinds of interference factors were considered, including the temperature, pH and organic solvents. By testing and recording their catalytic activity alterations, values of relative stability belonging to distinct mutants were calculated and compared with the values of initial wtMnP under identical treatment.
